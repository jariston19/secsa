# PAGE 1 — Post at door or check-in desk

## SECSA Exam Today

1. **Wait for staff** — you will be assigned a seat.
2. Open: **`http://_____________________:5173`**
3. **Log in** with your SECSA email and password.
4. **Do not click Begin Exam** until staff confirms you are ready.
5. **One browser tab only** — SECSA only. No phones. No other apps.
6. Problem? **Raise your hand** — do not refresh the page unless staff says so.

# PAGE 2 — Student desk card (cut, laminate, or place on each PC)

## SECSA — Quick steps

| Step | What to do |
|------|------------|
| **1** | Open **`http://_____________________:5173`** |
| **2** | Log in (email + password) |
| **3** | Click **Start** for your exam type |
| **4** | Read the instructions → check the box(es) → **Begin Exam** |
| **5** | When prompted: **fullscreen** (button or **F11**) → **Continue** |
| **6** | Answer all questions → **Submit Exam** (or wait for timer) |
| **7** | Wait until loading finishes — see your **result** or **profile** before closing |

### Rules

- **One question at a time** — your order is unique to you.
- **Timer starts** when you click **Begin Exam**.
- You may **change answers** until you submit or time runs out.
- **Two tab/window switches** allowed — exam pauses until you return.
- On the **third** switch, your exam **auto-submits**.
- After submit, answers are **final**.

### Incoming diagnostic only

- Check **two boxes**: instructions **and** privacy policy.
- You will **not** receive a grade — you will receive a **learning profile**.

### Comprehensive / retake / preboard only

- **Passing mark: 75%** (comprehensive and retake).
- Your **score and pass/fail** appear right after submit.

### Need help?

**Raise your hand.** Do not refresh unless staff tells you.

# PAGE 3 — Staff script (read aloud, ~2 minutes)

Say the same words to each student (or one pair). Then verify login before they begin.

---

### 1. What this is *(~15 seconds)*

> “This is the **[incoming diagnostic / comprehensive]** on SECSA. Questions are **one at a time**. Your **timer starts** when you click **Begin Exam**.”

---

### 2. The three rules *(~30 seconds)*

> “**One tab only** — SECSA only.”  
> “When asked, go **fullscreen** — **F11** or the button — then **Continue**.”  
> “You get **two tab switches**. On the **third**, the exam **auto-submits**.”

---

### 3. How to finish *(~15 seconds)*

> “Click **Submit Exam** when you are done. **Wait** until it finishes loading. **Do not close the browser** until you see your **result** or **profile**.”

---

### 4. If something breaks *(~15 seconds)*

> “**Raise your hand**. Do **not refresh** unless we tell you.”

---

### 5. Incoming diagnostic only *(~15 seconds)*

> “You will check **two boxes** — **instructions** and **privacy** — then **Begin Exam**. You will **not** get a grade. You will get a **learning profile**.”

---

### 6. Comprehensive only *(~15 seconds)*

> “Passing is **75%**. You will see your **score** right after you submit.”

---

### Release to start

> “**Log in now.** Tell me when you see the **Start Exam** screen.”

After they confirm the dashboard: assign seat → “You may **Begin** when you are seated.”

# PAGE 4 — Staff checklist (one person per room)

## Before doors open

- [ ] Server running — students can open the SECSA URL on room PCs
- [ ] Correct question set **deployed** for year + program
- [ ] Seat numbers posted (whiteboard or list)
- [ ] Desk cards placed at each PC (Page 2)
- [ ] Door sign posted (Page 1)

## Per walk-in student (~3–5 minutes)

- [ ] Name checked against list
- [ ] Seat assigned
- [ ] Desk card pointed out (or handed)
- [ ] **Staff script** read (Page 3) — or “read the card, I will confirm”
- [ ] Student **logged in** — **Start Exam** screen visible
- [ ] Student told they may click **Begin** when seated

## During the exam

- [ ] Watch for extra tabs / phones
- [ ] Remind about fullscreen if skipped
- [ ] Tab switch: warn at 1st and 2nd; 3rd is auto-submit (system enforced)
- [ ] “Submitting…” spinner — tell them to **wait**
- [ ] New walk-ins wait if you are helping someone on the floor
